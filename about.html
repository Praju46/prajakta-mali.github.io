About Me

Hi! I’m Prajakta Mali. Thank you for visiting my webpage.

I am a Remote Sensing and GIS Analyst with a strong academic background in geospatial analysis, urban and environmental studies, hydrology, and climate applications. I hold a Master’s degree in Remote Sensing and GIS from the Indian Institute of Remote Sensing (IIRS), ISRO, specializing in Urban and Regional Studies.

Currently, I am working as a Young Professional – WebGIS at the Maharashtra Institution for Transformation (MITRA), Government of Maharashtra. My work focuses on urban resilience, infrastructure planning, and WebGIS-based decision support systems.

Previously, I worked as a Junior Research Fellow at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bengaluru, where I contributed to river basin management, flood inundation mapping using Sentinel-1 SAR data, soil erosion modelling, and climate data analysis using Python.

My research interests include urban flood modelling, hydrological processes, land-use change analysis, climate change impacts, GIS automation, and WebGIS application development.
